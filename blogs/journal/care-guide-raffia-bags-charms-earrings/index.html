How to Care for Raffia Bags, Charms and Earrings

A raffia bag is a living thing - banana leaf, doum, glass beads, hand-crochet, every part worked between someone's fingers. Tend it the way Fatima tends hers in our Guéliz studio, and it keeps its shape, its colour, its story. Here is how we look after ours, told plainly.

Quick answer

Keep it dry. Keep its shape. Wipe it softly, and go gently around the beads and the handles. Hand-made is not the same as fragile - it means a hand that knew the work made it, and it deserves a hand that knows how to keep it.

Why care comes with the craft

Every YZA piece is woven in our Guéliz studio, by the women of the atelier - Fatima has had her hands in raffia for decades. A basket bag is roughly forty-eight hours of work, spread across about six days; a crocheted fruit charm can take her up to six hours for a single bunch of grapes - the cherries and grapes are shaped with l'aakad, the hand-braided caftan-button technique noted in the 2025 UNESCO file for the Moroccan caftan. When a thing is made that slowly, knot by knot, it asks for a little attention in return. That is all care is, really - keeping the work alive.

Keep it dry and shaped

Banana leaf and raffia are light, delicate natural fibres - they don't want to be soaked, and they don't want to be flattened under things in a drawer. Keep a bag away from heavy rain, pools, and the washing machine. If it does catch a shower, let it air-dry slowly on its own, away from direct heat and strong sun - banana leaf darkens in the sun, so the shade keeps the colour even. To store it, stand it upright or fill it loosely with tissue, so it holds the silhouette the hand gave it.

Clean gently

For a small mark, a dry soft cloth is usually all it takes; a barely damp one for something more stubborn, then leave it to dry. No bleach, no alcohol, no scrubbing, no machine - none of those is kind to a natural fibre. Around the charms and earrings, dab gently through the crochet rather than rubbing, and let the piece dry through completely before it goes back on the shelf.

Protect the details

The glass beads, the rings, the raffia-wrapped handles - these are the slow parts, so handle them slowly. Don't pull hard, don't twist, don't load a bag past what feels easy, and keep sharp objects away from the weave. A handle carried with a little care lasts; one yanked across a shoulder day after day gives out. Where there's leather, it gathers a patina - design with those changes, and the bag ages into your life, not away from it.

When something does need attention

Hand-made pieces are perfectly imperfect, and now and then a thread loosens or a bead shifts - that is the nature of the hand, not a fault in the work. A loose end can usually be eased back into place with a gentle hand. And when it needs more, bring it to us: we repair YZA pieces at the Guéliz studio for life, free, and ask only for shipping if you're sending one from far away. We made it, so we know how to keep it whole.
